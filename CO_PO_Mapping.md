## CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION

### 1.1 Background and Motivation
In the contemporary healthcare landscape, ensuring a steady and accessible supply of blood remains a critical challenge. Research consistently demonstrates that timely access to compatible blood products directly correlates with patient survival rates during medical emergencies, complex surgeries, and chronic treatments. However, traditional blood donation infrastructure often suffers from fragmentation, data latency, and severe communication barriers between willing donors and urgent recipients.

Currently, many regions rely on centralized blood banks that may not reflect real-time local availability or localized demand surges. When an emergency arises, families frequently resort to ad hoc requests on social media, which lack medical verification and geographic precision. This information gap leads to avoidable delays in critical care. **LifeStream AI** is conceived as a direct response to these challenges. By providing a unified, real-time platform, it empowers local communities to coordinate blood supply dynamically, ensuring that life-saving resources are always within reach.

### 1.2 Scope of the Project
**LifeStream AI** is scoped as a cross-platform mobile application designed for both Android and iOS ecosystems. The platform facilitates real-time coordination between two primary user classes: **Donors** and **Recipients**.

The scope encompasses five key functional pillars:
1. **Role-Based Onboarding**: Secure authentication and eligibility verification.
2. **Real-Time Emergency Radar**: Geolocation-based mapping within a 100km radius.
3. **AI Health Integration**: Personalized wellness coaching for donors and recovery care for recipients.
4. **Automated Broadcasting**: Instant push notifications dispatched to compatible local donors.
5. **Secure Communication**: Direct channels (Call/WhatsApp) for safe logistics coordination.

The project explicitly excludes commercial payment gateways and deep hospital inventory integrations to focus entirely on immediate, community-driven emergency logistics.

### 1.3 Technology Overview
The architecture of LifeStream AI utilizes a robust, modern technology stack selected for cross-platform performance. The mobile client is developed using **Flutter** and **Dart**. Application state logic is managed via **Flutter Riverpod** utilizing modern code generation. 

The backend infrastructure relies on **Firebase** (Authentication, Firestore, Storage, Messaging) for instant data propagation. Mapping interfaces use the **Google Maps SDK**, while intelligent capabilities are powered by the **Google Generative AI SDK (Gemini 1.5/3.1 Flash)**.

### 1.4 Alignment with Sustainable Development Goals
The platform addresses key global goals:
* **SDG 3 (Good Health & Well-being)**: Directly saving lives through rapid donor matching.
* **SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure)**: Deploying modern AI paradigms into healthcare logistics.
* **SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities)**: Democratizing access to emergency resources.

## CHAPTER 2: PROBLEM STATEMENT

### 2.1 Problem Definition
The management of emergency blood supply and real-time donor-recipient matching presents a multifaceted coordination challenge that affects two primary stakeholder groups: families or healthcare providers seeking urgent blood products, and eligible individuals willing to donate.

From a recipient perspective, the fundamental problem is one of extreme urgency compounded by systemic information fragmentation. When a medical emergency occurs, securing compatible blood within the critical "Golden Hour" requires immediate, localized communication. Currently, families frequently resort to unstructured broadcast messages on social media and messaging apps. This approach lacks verification, geographical precision, and real-time feedback. Recipients spend crucial hours cold-calling unvetted lists of past donors, often discovering too late that individuals are either too far away, unavailable, or medically ineligible.

From a donor perspective, the absence of centralized, intelligent tools creates friction that suppresses donation rates. Willing participants rarely know when a patient in their immediate vicinity requires their specific blood type. Furthermore, ambiguous health requirements, confusing eligibility windows, and lack of direct logistical coordination discourage potential lifesavers from acting.

Existing solutions in the market fail to provide a cohesive, modern mobile experience tailored for rapid emergency response. Centralized blood bank registries are static, poorly maintained, and do not reflect the immediate location of donors. Mapping tools lack medical intelligence. LifeStream AI addresses this gap by establishing a closed-loop system connecting precise geolocation algorithms, secure identity frameworks, and AI-assisted pre-screening.

### 2.2 Objectives
The primary objective of LifeStream AI is to design, build, and evaluate a highly responsive, cross-platform mobile application that securely automates real-time matching between blood donors and recipients. The specific objectives guiding implementation include:
* To develop a secure, role-aware authentication system distinguishing between Donors and Recipients using Firebase Auth.
* To create a real-time Emergency Radar module leveraging Google Maps API to visualize available donors within relevant proximity.
* To implement an intelligent pre-screening interface using Google Gemini AI, providing conversational validation of donor eligibility.
* To deploy an automated cloud broadcasting service that instantly routes SOS notifications to compatible peers.
* To build encrypted peer-to-peer communication channels for logistical coordination.
* To establish a clean, scalable MVVM architecture utilizing Flutter Riverpod for optimal state isolation.

### 2.3 Research Questions
* How can serverless NoSQL backends (Firestore) replace legacy relational databases for sub-second emergency geolocation queries?
* What application state patterns effectively prevent excessive API re-renders when updating interactive mapping markers dynamically?
* How can conversational Large Language Models be effectively constrained to follow official WHO/Red Cross medical eligibility guidelines?

## CHAPTER 3: ANALYSIS

### 3.1 Software Requirement Specifications (SRS)
The Software Requirement Specification serves as the primary blueprint for system implementation. It documents the specific operational capabilities, quality expectations, and technical bounds required to deploy LifeStream AI reliably in high-stakes emergency healthcare scenarios.

#### 3.1.1 Functional Requirements
The system requirements are grouped logically by mobile application modules:
* **User Management Module**: Users shall be able to securely register, authenticate, and recover accounts using verified credentials via Firebase Auth. Profiles must support distinct role assignment (Donor or Recipient), precise blood group validation (A+, A-, etc.), and secure storage of contact numbers.
* **Emergency Radar Module**: The platform shall compute real-time distances using device coordinates. Recipients shall be able to visualize active donors on an interactive Google Map within a targeted 100km coverage zone.
* **AI Health Coach Module**: The application shall interface with the Gemini API to guide users through basic wellness protocols. Donors shall be able to converse regarding donation recovery, while recipients obtain basic post-transfusion diet advice.
* **SOS Broadcast Module**: Recipients in critical need shall possess the capability to initiate a "Broadcast SOS." The platform will query active Firestore documents to identify compatible local users and immediately route transactional alerts.
* **Secure Communication Module**: Validated peers shall gain access to designated chat or voice routing capabilities without compromising raw database keys.

#### 3.1.2 Non-Functional Requirements
* **Performance**: Real-time map updates must re-render seamlessly, utilizing Riverpod to minimize frame drops.
* **Security**: Health metrics and location trails must adhere strictly to end-to-end encryption protocols. 
* **Reliability**: Fallback queries shall trigger seamlessly if satellite GPS connectivity degrades briefly.

### 3.2 Feasibility Study
#### 3.2.1 Technical Feasibility
The proposed system leverages a highly resilient, industrial-grade mobile development ecosystem backed by comprehensive community resources. Flutter and Dart deliver a unified codebase paradigm, compiling natively into ARM binaries across both Android and iOS to eliminate cross-platform UI discrepancies. Firebase serves as the optimized Backend-as-a-Service (BaaS), providing battle-tested scalability for real-time synchronization, profile authentication, and document retrieval without demanding localized server maintenance. Accessing advanced intelligent capabilities remains seamlessly achievable via official Google Generative AI client packages. Consequently, implementation thresholds are securely bounded and evaluated as technically viable.

#### 3.2.2 Economic Feasibility
Development activities leverage entirely open-source SDKs, ensuring zero capital expenditure related to enterprise IDE licensing or proprietary runtime permissions. Deployment workflows operate seamlessly under free tier cloud boundaries, encompassing Firebase storage allocations and base Gemini token allowances. Should widespread adoption materialize, localized pay-as-you-go scaling protects operational budgets effectively.

#### 3.2.3 Operational Feasibility
The interface architecture adopts clean mobile heuristics familiar to a broad user base. One-touch broadcast activation handles immediate SOS logic smoothly.

### 3.3 Tools, Technologies and Platforms
#### 3.3.1 Frontend Stack
Built exclusively on the Flutter framework utilizing compiled Dart runtimes. State persistence is enforced leveraging code-generated Riverpod instances.

#### 3.3.2 Backend Technologies (Firebase)
Firebase serves as the complete managed backend ecosystem for LifeStream AI, replacing the administrative overhead of deploying custom Node.js server environments. The persistence architecture utilizes Cloud Firestore, an enterprise NoSQL document store logically organized into distributed collections including `users`, `blood_requests`, `active_broadcasts`, and `secure_chats`. Client queries access datasets securely using the `cloud_firestore` SDK. Identity verification relies entirely on Firebase Auth to handle encrypted JWT lifecycle transitions.

#### 3.3.3 Development and Deployment Infrastructure
Code integrity is managed via standard Git protocols mapped to GitHub endpoints. Build constraints mask production keys safely using standard `flutter_dotenv` frameworks.
